Essential Volleyball Skills

Success in volleyball requires mastering several fundamental skills. Whether you're a beginner or an advanced player, focusing on proper technique is key to improving your game.

Players executing different volleyball skills during a competitive match

Core Volleyball Skills

  • Serving
    • Underhand Serve: Basic serve for beginners
    • Overhand Float Serve: Advanced serve with minimal spin
    • Topspin Serve: Powerful serve with downward movement
    • Jump Serve: Advanced attacking serve with approach jump
  • Passing
    • Forearm Pass: Basic platform passing technique
    • Overhead Pass: Using fingers to direct the ball
    • Dig: Defensive pass to save attacked balls
  • Setting
    • Front Set: Standard set to attackers
    • Back Set: Set behind the setter
    • Jump Set: Setting while jumping for faster offense
  • Attacking
    • Spike: Powerful overhead attack
    • Tip/Dink: Soft placement attack
    • Roll Shot: High-arcing off-speed attack
  • Blocking
    • Single Block: One defender at the net
    • Double Block: Two defenders working together
    • Triple Block: Three defenders against powerful attacks

Training Tips for Key Skills

Improve your volleyball skills with these expert tips and techniques:

  • For Better Passing: Create a flat platform with your arms, keep your eyes on the ball, and move your feet to get in position early.
  • For Accurate Setting: Use your fingertips (not palms), square your shoulders to your target, and follow through toward your target.
  • For Powerful Attacking: Approach with quick steps, jump vertically rather than horizontally, and snap your wrist upon contact.
  • For Effective Blocking: Watch the hitter's shoulder and arm swing rather than the ball, press over the net, and seal the edges.
  • For Consistent Serving: Develop a consistent pre-serve routine, focus on a specific target, and maintain a relaxed but firm wrist.

Drill of the Month

Triangle Passing Drill

Improve passing accuracy and movement with this simple but effective drill:

  1. Form groups of three players in a triangle formation
  2. One player tosses the ball to another
  3. The receiver passes to the third player
  4. Continue rotating after each pass
  5. Focus on proper form and accurate passing

Advanced Techniques

Once you've mastered the basics, these advanced techniques can take your game to the next level:

Jump Float Serve

An advanced serve that combines a jump with a floating ball motion, making it difficult to predict and pass.

  • Toss the ball slightly in front of you
  • Take a quick approach jump
  • Contact the ball with a firm wrist
  • Minimize spin for unpredictable movement

Back Row Attack

An attacking option for back row players that requires precise footwork and timing.

  • Start your approach from behind the attack line
  • Time your jump to contact the ball at peak height
  • Land before touching the attack line
  • Use a powerful swing with topspin

Slide Attack

An attacking approach for middle hitters that involves a curved approach along the net.

  • Start your approach from the middle of the court
  • Take a curved path toward the setter
  • Time your jump to meet the set away from the net
  • Attack at the peak of your jump
