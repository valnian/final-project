Volleyball Rules & Regulations

Learn the official rules of volleyball for both indoor and beach variations. Understand scoring, positions, and gameplay fundamentals.

Basic Volleyball Rules

Volleyball is played by two teams of six players on a court divided by a net. The objective is to ground the ball on the opponent's side of the court or force them to commit a fault.

Standard volleyball court setup with players in position

Key Gameplay Rules

  • Starting the Game
    • The game begins with a serve from behind the end line
    • The serving team is determined by a coin toss
  • Scoring System
    • Rally scoring: point awarded on every serve
    • Sets are played to 25 points (must win by 2 points)
    • Match is typically best of 5 sets
  • Player Rotation
    • Players rotate clockwise when gaining serve
    • Each player must be in correct position at time of serve
  • Legal Plays
    • Maximum of three touches per side
    • A player cannot hit the ball twice consecutively
    • The ball must be clearly hit, not caught or thrown

Indoor vs. Beach Volleyball Rules

While the basic concepts are similar, indoor and beach volleyball have several important rule differences:

Rule Aspect Indoor Volleyball Beach Volleyball
Players per Team 6 players 2 players
Court Size 18m x 9m 16m x 8m
Scoring Best of 5 sets to 25 Best of 3 sets to 21
Libero Player Allowed (defensive specialist) Not allowed
Open-hand Tips Allowed Not allowed (must hard-driven)
Block Touch Does not count as first touch Counts as first touch
Setting Standards More lenient Strict (no double touches)

Common Violations and Faults

Understanding common violations will help you avoid penalties and improve your gameplay:

  • Foot Fault: Stepping on or over the line while serving
  • Double Hit: contacting the ball twice in succession
  • Lift/Carry: catching or throwing the ball instead of hitting it
  • Four Hits: using more than three touches before returning the ball
  • Net Violation: touching the net during play
  • Center Line Violation: completely crossing the center line
  • Rotation Fault: players not in correct position at service
  • Back Row Attack: back row player attacking from front zone

Proper net play: players must avoid touching the net during attacks and blocks

Officiating and Signals

Volleyball matches are officiated by a team of referees who use specific hand signals to communicate decisions:

First Referee

The first referee stands at one end of the net and has ultimate authority over the match.

  • Controls the match from start to finish
  • Makes final decisions on all rules
  • Can overrule other officials

Second Referee

Standing opposite the first referee, the second referee assists with calls.

  • Monitors net violations and center line faults
  • Watches for rotational faults
  • Assists with substitution management

Line Judges

Typically two or four line judges monitor boundary lines.

  • Determine if balls are in or out
  • Watch for foot faults on service
  • Use flags to signal decisions
